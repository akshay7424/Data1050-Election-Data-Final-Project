The project explores the potential success of startup companies in the U.S. by examining key features that economically and financially impact these companies. Currently, venture capital firms use intuition, financial projections, and personal connections to determine which companies receive seed money. Understandably, there is high risk and uncertainty associated with venture capital where only a handful of companies become unicorns (> $1bn valuation) with successful exits. The goal of this machine learning project is to shed some light into what variables contribute to a company’s success, outside of financials, to reduce the randomness and personal bias in venture capital investment.

SVC

k-NN classification - We will use k-NN classification to determine whether the dataset has any predictive power regarding a startup company’s success. Accuracy score and precision-recall will be ideal evaluation metrics since this is a classification problem. Additionally, a confusion matrix can be used to visualize the misclassification error.

Random forest - We will compute a large number of individual decision trees that operate as an ensemble, where each individual tree in the forest spits out a class prediction and class with the highest probability of success becomes our model’s prediction. We will test the accuracy of the model taking note that usually random forest regression models are overfitting.
